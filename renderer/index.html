CFB Transfer Draft Class Studio

Dashboard

Pull departing players from your CFB 27 dynasty, generate a calibrated Madden draft class, and write it into your franchise save.

1 · Player Pool

Load the players leaving your CFB dynasty via college save file, or a CSV export.

Your CFB save MUST be Offseason Week 1 (the "Draft Stage"). This is the one point in a dynasty where the game has finalized exactly who's declaring for the draft. It cannot be any earlier or later week — other weeks either don't have this data yet or have already moved past it, and will give wrong or incomplete results.

No direct save access? Export your roster to a CSV instead (must include at minimum FirstName, LastName, Position, OverallRating columns) and load that.

2 · Generate Class

Applies every setting from Position Weights, Physical Attributes, and Advanced to the loaded pool.

Recommended: generate once, then move on. Each run adds a little randomness on top of your settings (that's intentional — real draft classes have that same natural variance), so re-running produces a slightly different class every time. Only regenerate repeatedly if you're specifically testing how a Position Weight or Physical Attribute change affects the output on the Draft Class page — otherwise, generate once and write it into your franchise.

3 · Write to Madden Franchise

Overwrites the incoming rookie class in your Madden 26 franchise save with your generated class.

Your Madden save CANNOT be in Preseason. The incoming rookie draft class doesn't exist yet during preseason, so there's nothing there for this tool to overwrite. Use a save from Regular Season, Postseason, or a later Offseason stage — anything except Preseason.

Position Weights

        

          Every college player's ratings get scaled down before entering Madden,
          so freshmen don't show up as 99-overall superstars. This page lets you
          nudge how strong or weak that scaling is, position by position.
        

        

          
        

      

      

        
Extra Drop — think of this as a "toughness dial" for the
        position. It's a number of extra rating points subtracted from
        every single rating a player at this position has (Speed,
        Awareness, Route Running, everything), on top of the normal calibration.
        A higher number means players at this position come into Madden weaker
        across the board. A lower number (or negative) means they keep more of
        their college rating. Example: setting Wide Receiver's Extra Drop to 5
        means every generated WR ends up about 5 points lower on every rating
        than they otherwise would.

        
Class Cap — the maximum number of players at this
        position allowed into the generated draft class at all, no matter how
        many are eligible in your CFB save. Leave blank for no limit. This
        exists mainly because CFB tends to produce far more Kickers, Punters,
        and Long Snappers leaving than a real NFL draft class would ever have
        room for.

Physical Attributes

        

          "Physical" ratings — Speed, Strength, Acceleration, Agility, Jumping,
          and similar — are handled differently than skill ratings elsewhere in
          this app. A player's raw athletic testing translates fairly directly
          from college to the pros, so instead of complex remapping, these just
          get a straightforward point reduction. This page controls how big
          that reduction is.
        

        

          
        

      


      

        
Global

        
Drop Leniency — the master strength dial for
        every rating reduction in this app, both the flat drops on this
        page and the extra squeeze applied to skill ratings on the Advanced
        page. At 1.0, reductions are at full calibrated strength. At the
        default 0.25, players only lose about a quarter of that — ratings stay
        much closer to their original college values. Turn this up if generated
        classes feel too strong/unrealistic; turn it down if they feel too weak.

        
Default Drop — a fallback only. Physical ratings are
        normally reduced using real calibration data built from actual Madden
        rosters, position by position. If a specific position/rating
        combination has no calibration data on file, this flat number is used
        instead. You generally won't need to touch this.

        
Physical Jitter — adds a small amount of random
        variation (plus or minus this many points) to physical ratings after
        the drop is applied, so two CFB players with identical ratings don't
        come out as identical Madden players. Bigger number = more
        player-to-player variety.

        
Skill Jitter — the same idea as Physical Jitter, but
        applied to skill ratings instead (Awareness, route running, coverage,
        blocking, etc. — anything not in the table below).

        

      


      

        
Per-Rating Adjustments

        

          Fine-tune individual physical ratings beyond the global settings above.
        

        
    
          
  • Extra Drop — extra points subtracted from just
          this one rating, for every player, on top of everything else. Leave
          at the default unless you have a specific reason to change it.
    • 
          
  • Jitter Override — by default, a rating uses the
          global Physical Jitter value above. Enter a number here to give just
          this rating its own random-variance width instead (e.g. Agility
          defaults to a wider jitter than other ratings, so it varies more from
          player to player). Leave blank to just use the global value.
    • 
          
  • Max Drop — a hard ceiling on the total amount
          this rating can be reduced, no matter how much the other settings add
          up to. Agility, for example, is capped at 7 by default, so no player's
          Agility can ever be cut by more than 7 points even if jitter and extra
          drop stack higher. Leave blank for no ceiling.

Advanced

        
Deeper settings for how large the class is, how skill ratings get shaped, and how development traits are handed out. These have more subtle effects than Position Weights or Physical Attributes — read the descriptions before changing them.

        

          
        

      


      

        
Class Construction

        

          Class Size caps how many players get pulled into the
          generated class, chosen from the best available prospects in your CFB
          save (ranked by their CFB projected draft round, then overall). If
          your dynasty has fewer players leaving than this number, you'll just
          get fewer — it's a ceiling, not a target. Seed leave
          blank for a different random class every time (recommended). Type any
          word or number to make results reproducible — the exact same seed,
          settings, and CFB save will always produce an identical class, which
          is useful when comparing two configurations without random variance
          also changing things. K/P Awareness Cap — Awareness
          represents football IQ built up over a career, which real rookie
          kickers/punters haven't had time to develop regardless of how CFB
          rates them; this caps how high their Awareness can be set.
        

        

      


      

        
Bell-Curve Squeeze skill ratings

        

          Skill ratings (Awareness, route running, coverage, blocking, etc.)
          aren't just shifted down by a flat amount. First, each player is
          matched to the same percentile rank in a database of real Madden
          draft classes — "this player is better than 80% of college players
          at his position" makes him as good as the real player sitting at that
          same 80th percentile in actual Madden data. Then, an extra "squeeze"
          is layered on top, strongest at one chosen percentile of the class and
          fading out toward both the elite prospects and the replacement-level
          ones. These four settings control that squeeze.
        

        
    
          
  • Peak Percentile — which percentile of players
          gets squeezed hardest, from 0 (worst at the position) to 1 (best).
          The default 0.75 means solid-but-not-elite prospects (around the 75th
          percentile) take the biggest extra hit, while true elites and
          low-end depth are mostly left alone.
    • 
          
  • Peak Extra Drop — how many extra points get
          subtracted right at that peak percentile, on top of whatever the
          percentile-matching step already calculated.
    • 
          
  • Spread Below Peak — how far the squeeze reaches
          into players ranked below the peak. Bigger = a wider range
          of below-peak players get some extra reduction; smaller = the squeeze
          stays narrowly focused right at the peak and fades out fast.
    • 
          
  • Spread Above Peak — the same idea, but for
          players ranked above the peak (closer to elite). Kept narrow
          by default on purpose, so truly elite prospects barely get touched —
          the goal is protecting top talent while trimming the "good but not
          great" tier just below it.
    • 
        

        

      


      

        
Development Traits

        

          Controls how Madden's development traits (Normal, Star, Superstar,
          X-Factor — from most to least common) get handed out across the
          generated class. Traits affect how fast a player improves and, for
          Superstar/X-Factor, unlock special in-game abilities.
        

        
    
          
  • X-Factor Min Overall — the CFB overall rating a
          player must reach to automatically receive the rare X-Factor trait,
          Madden's highest tier. Keep this high (97+) — it should only catch
          genuine outlier prospects.
    • 
          
  • Superstar Count — exactly this many players (the
          best remaining by CFB overall, after X-Factor is handled) will
          receive the Superstar trait, Madden's second-highest tier.
    • 
          
  • Star Target % — the target share of the
          entire generated class that should end up with the Star
          trait (one tier below Superstar). Every player CFB already tagged
          "College Elite" is included automatically first; then the best
          remaining "College Star" players are added one at a time until this
          percentage is reached. Everyone else gets the baseline Normal
          trait.
